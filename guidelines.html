Guidelines

• Wash your hands or use hand sanitizer before wearing your mask

• Wear your mask over the nose and mouth

• You are required to wear your mask in any form of public transportation.

Click here for more information about masks


• Getting vaccinated will help reduce your chances from being affected

• Once you take the vaccine, you'll able to do some activities that you weren't able to do during the pandemic.

Click here for more information about vaccines

• Be six feet away from those who are sick and do not live in your household

• Remember that six feet distance is around two arm lengths.

Click here for more information about social distancing


• Wash your hands with soap and water for a minimum of 20 seconds

• Use hand sanitizer (60% alcohol) if soap and water is not avaivable

• Do not touch your eyes, nose, and mouth if you did not wash your hands

Click here for more information about handwashing

• First, use cleaning products like detergent or soap and water

• Then, use household disinfectants

• Clean and disinfect items that are touched freqently like doorknobs, tables, light switches, or desks.

Click here for more information on cleaning and disinfecting.

• Use your a tissue or an elbow when covering your coughs or sneezes

• Throw these tissues in the trash

• Immediately wash your hands after

• Check if you have any of the following symptoms mentioned on our Symptoms Page

• If you experience any of the following symptoms, check your temperature

• Read the CDC Guidance page if you experience any of the following symptoms

Additional COVID-19 Guidelines

• Stay at home unless necessary

• Stay away from crowds, gatherings

• If you're not feeling well, you should remain in home and get medical care

• Follow quarantine guidelines
