Guidelines

• Wash your hands or use hand sanitizer before wearing your mask.

• Wear your mask over the nose and mouth.

• You are required to wear your mask in any form of public transportation.

Click here for more information about masks.


• Getting vaccinated will help reduce your chances of being infected.

• Once you take the vaccine, you will be able to partake in activities previously forbidden during the pandemic.

Click here for more information about vaccines.

• Stay six feet away from other people, especially those who are sick and do not live in your household.

• Remember that six feet is approximately two arm lengths.

Click here for more information about social distancing.


• Wash your hands with soap and water for a minimum of 20 seconds.

• Use hand sanitizer (60% alcohol) if soap and water are not available.

• Refrain from touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.

Click here for more information about handwashing.

• Use cleaning products like detergent, soap and water, or household disinfectants.

• Clean and disinfect items that are frequently touched like doorknobs, tables, light switches, or desks.

Click here for more information on cleaning and disinfecting.

• Use a tissue or your elbow when covering your coughs or sneezes.

• Immediately throw these tissues in the trash and wash your hands.

• Check if you have any of the following symptoms mentioned on our Symptoms Page or fill out our Questionnaire.

• If you experience any of the symptoms, check your temperature.

Read the CDC Guidance page if you experience any COVID-19 symptoms.

Additional COVID-19 Guidelines:

• Stay at home unless it is necessary to leave.

• Stay away from crowds and large gatherings.

• If you are not feeling well, remain at home and receive medical attention.

• Follow quarantine guidelines listed here.
